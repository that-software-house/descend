### ISSUE #1 — CONTINUITY BREAK AT the_construct: ATROPHY AS NEW INFORMATION
**Node:** `the_construct`
**Type:** Continuity break / Orphaned emotional state
**Description:** The construct node's text says "Your muscles have atrophied. Your eyes have never focused in real light before." But by this point the player has just spent eight nodes (`awakening_eyes_open` through `awakening_mirror_response`) processing exactly this physical reality in exhaustive, emotionally resonant detail. Neo has already been told he was in for twenty-six years, has already failed to sit up from the floor, has already looked at his white hands in the mirror. The construct's mention of atrophy treats it as fresh information when it has already been the dominant emotional through-line for the past chapter. This also implies a sequencing error: the construct text seems to have been written as if it follows the unplugging directly, before the awakening sequence existed.
**Suggested fix:** Replace the atrophy reference in `the_construct` with something that bridges out of the awakening sequence emotionally: "Everything you learned about your body in the last hours — Tank is offering you a place where none of that matters. Not yet. The white is freedom from the failing flesh." This acknowledges the awakening while motivating the construct's appeal.

### ISSUE #2 — ORPHANED NODE: the_oracle (UNREACHABLE)
**Node:** `the_oracle`
**Type:** Dead end (inverse — a node that exists but is never reachable)
**Description:** The node `the_oracle` exists in the JSON and contains a coherent, meta-aware Oracle scene where she breaks the fourth wall ("She looks at you. Not at the character. Not at Thomas Anderson. At you. 'You've been here before,' she says quietly. 'Haven't you.'"). However, no other node in the graph has a `next` pointer to `the_oracle`. It is completely unreachable through any path. It appears to be a legacy draft of the Oracle encounter that was replaced by the richer `oracle_arrival` → `oracle_waiting_room` → `oracle_kitchen` chain, but was never removed. This is a significant orphaned node.

Additionally, `the_oracle`'s choices both jump directly to `morpheus_captured`, skipping the entire construct and training sequences. If this node were ever made reachable, it would create a severe continuity break (arriving at "the call comes in the middle of a training run" without having done any training).

**Suggested fix:** Either (a) remove `the_oracle` node entirely, or (b) repurpose it as a meta-layer unlocked on a repeat playthrough by the loop system — its fourth-wall-breaking quality makes it interesting as an alternate Oracle access for players who have looped. If kept, its choices should NOT skip to `morpheus_captured` without at minimum passing through `the_construct`.

### ISSUE #3 — CONTINUITY BREAK: morpheus_captured TEXT CONTRADICTS ITS INCOMING PATHS
**Node:** `morpheus_captured`
**Type:** Continuity break
**Description:** The node's opening line is "The call comes in the middle of a training run." This text was written assuming Neo is still in active training when Morpheus is captured. However, the two incoming paths that lead to `morpheus_captured` from the Oracle storyline are:
- `cypher_jackIn_moment` (c2: "Let it go. He plugs you in.") — this is the jack-in immediately following the Oracle visit and the Cypher confrontation. The player is not in a training run; they are beginning a Matrix mission.
- `cypher_jackIn_confront` (c1: "Enter the Matrix.") — same scenario.

In both cases, the player has completed the Oracle visit and is jacking in for the Morpheus rescue mission. The "middle of a training run" framing is a continuity break — it positions the betrayal as happening during routine training when narrative context makes clear Neo is jacking in for an active operation. In the film, the team jacks in together for the Oracle visit; on the way out, Cypher murders the crew and gives Morpheus to the Agents. The game's in-Matrix Oracle visit is separate from a "training run."

**Suggested fix:** Change the opening to: "You come back through the Oracle's neighborhood and the connection is wrong." Or: "The call comes the moment you're back in the Matrix. Cypher. It was Cypher." Removing the "training run" framing eliminates the contradiction without losing any narrative weight.

### ISSUE #4 — MISSING TEXTURE: lobby_upper_floor GHOST ROUTE (TEXT INTRUSION)
**Node:** `lobby_upper_floor`
**Type:** Tone mismatch / Missing texture
**Description:** The node's text contains an in-world narrative line that reads like a game-design comment rather than prose: "If flags.physicsGap and flags.trainingSlow — you can see it." This is raw conditional logic that has leaked into the narrative text field. It breaks the fourth wall accidentally (as opposed to the Oracle's intentional meta-awareness) and exposes implementation details to the player. The prose around it is good — "you can see it the way you see code" — but the condition statement itself is a fragment of system logic, not story text.
**Suggested fix:** Remove the `"If flags.physicsGap and flags.trainingSlow — you can see it."` line from the text array entirely. The game engine should handle the conditional display of the `lobby_ghost_route` choice based on those flags without narrating the condition. The surrounding text ("The floor plan has a pattern... you can see it the way you see code") already communicates the same idea without the exposed logic.

### ISSUE #5 — LORE CONTRADICTION: agent_smith IS NOT ON THE ROOF
**Node:** `agent_smith`
**Type:** Lore contradiction
**Description:** The node places the Smith confrontation "on the roof" and uses the film's dialogue ("Do you hear that? That is the sound of inevitability. It is the sound of your death."). In the film, this exchange happens in the subway tunnel, with Smith holding Neo against the tracks while a train approaches — the "inevitability" is the oncoming train. On a rooftop with city below, the line is present but its meaning is severed. The "sound of inevitability" loses its concrete referent (the train) and becomes a generic villain boast. This is both a setting error and a consequence-of-setting meaning collapse.

The rooftop makes sense as a location for a climactic fight (and the film does have rooftop sequences during the chase scenes), but transplanting the subway dialogue to the roof without acknowledging the referent mismatch creates a lore contradiction in the writing, not just the geography.
**Suggested fix:** Either (a) keep the rooftop but write original dialogue for Smith that fits the aerial/open setting, reserving the "sound of inevitability" speech for a subway version of this node if one is created; or (b) set this confrontation in a subway tunnel and restore the original context (the train, the grip, the neck pin). The "think about spoons" closing line works in either setting and should be kept.

### ISSUE #6 — MISSING TEXTURE: agent_smith CANONICAL CHOICE SKIPS DEATH AND RESURRECTION
**Node:** `agent_smith`
**Type:** Missing texture / Character logic break
**Description:** Choice c1 — "My name... is Neo." with condition `belief >= 2` — jumps directly to `ending_escape`. In the film, this line is spoken in the subway during the fight with Smith, at which point Neo is then hit by the train, apparently dying. He is subsequently killed by Agent Smith in the hallway, and Trinity's "I believe" speech and her kiss precede his resurrection as The One. The game's choice collapses this entire arc — death, Trinity's love confession, resurrection, bullet-stopping — into a single statement that somehow teleports to the escape ending.

The "My name is Neo" moment in the film is not the moment of triumph — it is the moment of defiance before apparent defeat. The triumph comes later, after death and resurrection. By making it the winning move that immediately triggers the escape ending, the game grants Neo transcendence at precisely the moment where the film's Neo was about to be killed.

This is the most significant missing texture in the canonical path, skipping what is arguably the most emotionally important sequence in the film.
**Suggested fix:** Re-route the "My name is Neo" choice to a node that depicts the subway death and subsequent resurrection sequence (even in abbreviated form), leading through Trinity's declaration before arriving at `ending_escape`. Alternatively, reserve this choice for the `the_one_moment` branch (which already handles the transcendence theme correctly) and reframe the `agent_smith` c1 choice as a moment of defiance that leads to the death/resurrection path.

### ISSUE #7 — CONTINUITY BREAK: the_calculation → the_phone (DEFEATED SMITH, BUT AGENT STILL IN PURSUIT)
**Node:** `the_phone` (incoming from `the_calculation`)
**Type:** Continuity break
**Description:** The `the_calculation` node ends with Neo defeating Smith: "The fight is short after that... The sound he makes is not a human sound. He staggers. You don't." The implication is that Smith has been incapacitated. The next node, `the_phone`, opens with: "The phone is ringing. An Agent is 40 feet behind you and closing." This creates a direct continuity break — the player has just won the fight against Smith, but is now being chased by a fresh Agent as though the fight never happened (or as though a different Agent was never engaged). Smith being replaced by a new Agent body (as happens in the film) is not explained or acknowledged.

**Suggested fix:** Insert a brief bridging sentence at the top of `the_phone` when reached from `the_calculation`: something like "Smith staggers and does not rise. But the simulation has others. Another Agent enters through the body of a passerby—" This uses the film's canon (Agents can take over any wired-in human body) to explain the new pursuer and eliminate the contradiction.

### ISSUE #8 — ORPHANED NODE: loop_02 (UNREACHABLE)
**Node:** `loop_02`
**Type:** Dead end (inverse — node exists but is never reached)
**Description:** The node `loop_02` exists in the JSON (it depicts Neo waking in his apartment, taking the blue pill, feeling "the absence of something you can't name — like a tooth that stopped aching") and has a single choice leading to `blue_wakeup`. However, no other node in the graph contains `"next": "loop_02"`. It is completely unreachable. The node has a `glitch: "subtle"` property and serves a narrative purpose (the amnesia of choosing the blue pill), but it is cut off from the graph.
**Suggested fix:** Connect `loop_02` from the `blue_wakeup` blue-pill choice flow. Alternatively, connect it as a destination from `the_offer` → blue pill choice (currently going directly to `blue_wakeup`), as an intermediate "loop state" before the full blue-pill awakening. Its content (the lingering wrongness after taking the blue pill) is too good to leave orphaned.

### ISSUE #9 — FALSE CHOICE: the_phone CHOICE C2 IS NOT A REAL CHOICE
**Node:** `the_phone`
**Type:** Choice imbalance / False choice
**Description:** The two options in `the_phone` are:
- "Answer it." → `ending_escape`
- "You freeze. You cannot make it." → `ending_captured`

The second option is labeled from Neo's internal experience, not as an active player choice — "You freeze" is a statement of involuntary failure, not a decision. No player who has invested in this playthrough would willingly choose to be captured. The framing also grammatically treats the player as someone who has already failed rather than offering a genuine fork.

More significantly: if the player has been on the canonical path through the Oracle, trained with Morpheus, warned Trinity about Cypher, and survived the lobby — the idea that Neo "cannot make it" to the phone is emotionally incoherent. There is no scenario in this Neo's journey where he freezes at the last moment, unless the game has tracked a low belief score.

**Suggested fix:** Either (a) gate the captured outcome behind a low-belief condition, so only players with insufficient belief accumulation can reach it; or (b) reframe the second choice as something that reflects an actual decision: "Turn back. You can't risk being killed when Morpheus still needs you." This preserves the captured outcome while making it feel like a choice rather than a failure state the player selects voluntarily.

### ISSUE #10 — CHARACTER LOGIC BREAK: morpheus_captured ALLOWS "ABORT" AS FINAL OPTION
**Node:** `morpheus_captured`
**Type:** Character logic break
**Description:** Choice c4 in `morpheus_captured` is: "It's too dangerous. We abort." → `ending_captured`. This leads to the captured ending, which depicts Neo being "reintegrated" as Thomas A. Anderson back at his cubicle. But the logic break is this: Neo would not choose to abort the rescue. In every version of Neo's character — the film, the game's own construction of him — the core motivation for going after Morpheus is that Morpheus believed in Neo first ("He believed in me first"). The Oracle has just confirmed Morpheus will sacrifice his life. Trinity has just confirmed Neo's suspicion about Cypher. Neo has just jacked back in knowing something is wrong.

The abort option is not in Neo's emotional vocabulary at this point. There is nothing in the game's own construction of the character that leads to "it's too dangerous." Even a cowardly Neo would need a far more specific scaffold — perhaps a node depicting Morpheus explicitly releasing Neo from the obligation — to make this choice coherent.

Additionally, if Neo aborts and ends up captured/reintegrated, it means the abort was the thing that got him caught, which is both ironic and unexplained by the ending text.

**Suggested fix:** Replace "abort" with a choice that has genuine moral weight, something Neo might actually grapple with: "Wait. If we go in now, they might already have the codes." This creates a brief beat of tactical hesitation before Neo commits, or could lead to a short scene where Tank tells him the codes will be extracted within the hour, forcing the choice back toward action. The "too dangerous" option works better as a path for Niobe-era Neo (who is strategic and risk-calculating) than this game's Neo.

## GRAPH-LEVEL OBSERVATIONS

**Orphaned nodes confirmed:** `the_oracle`, `loop_02` — both unreachable through any current path.

**All other `next` pointers resolve to valid node IDs.** No true dead ends (broken links to non-existent nodes) were found.

**Strongest sections of the graph:**
- The entire awakening sequence (`awakening_eyes_open` through `awakening_mirror_response`) is the game's best writing. It is patient, physically specific, and emotionally coherent.
- The Oracle sequence (`oracle_arrival` through `oracle_must_save_morpheus`) is the most faithful adaptation of the source material. The Oracle's voice is exactly right.
- The Cypher observation arc (`cypher_unease_ship`, `cypher_watch`, `cypher_find_trinity`) does something the film does not — it gives the player the chance to detect Cypher before the betrayal, which creates genuine player agency without breaking canon.
- The `the_one_moment` branch (conditional on belief >= 4) is the game's most ambitious writing — the bullet-stopping, the confrontation with Smith-as-program, the three endings. This section earns its existence.

**The blue-pill alternate timeline** is impressively constructed. The five-year drift into domesticity, the dream that won't stop, the revelation that comes too late — `blue_late_awakening` and `the_unplugging_late` are genuinely moving. The line "Trinity is older too. There are lines around her eyes that weren't there before. She doesn't say welcome. She says: 'You're here.' Like that's enough. Like that might actually be enough." is the best writing in the entire file.
